FAQ

Curious to find out more? You've come to the right place

Why do you create webpages and edit videos?

Because I have a lifelong love for creating things.

When I was a child, I used to write short stories, create my own board games, and build communities with Legos. As I grew up, I listened to songs and imagine stories within them that could be told with video clips of popular movies and TV shows. I felt compelled to tell my stories so I became a video editor. My work expanded and includes videos currently used for training by the US Department of Homeland Security.

Presently, I'm training to become a web developer because I am full of ideas for websites and mobile apps that I want to turn into a reality.

How did you become a video editor?

In 2007, I taught myself how to use iMovie and created my first video, A Tribute to Women in Film, which I scored with Bob Dylan's Lay Lady Lay.

While satisifed with my first production, I later saw things I wanted to change. At the same time, I started experimenting more and more with iMovie's features and adapted a style of editing that was in sync with the rhythm of the music. With each successive video I created, I used a new feature and tightened how I used cuts and transitions.

In 2009, I was hired for my first professional job. Whether I create a professional video for a client or a personal video of my own ideas, I find the process a labor of love and take great satisfaction when I see the final product!

How are you training to become a web developer?

I am enrolled in CareerFoundry's Web Development program. Currently, I spend about 20 hours each week completing exercises such as building webpages from scratch and adding features. Within 6 months, I will have created at least two professional websites.

What other work have you performed?

I've had a very diverse career. In eleven years, I've held the following jobs:


  • Covered night shifts at a psychiatric crisis center
  • Served as a case manager for 16 men with severe mental illness. Monitored my clients' psychiatric functioning and ensured that they received their medications, entitlements, and proper medical & psychiatric treatment
  • Secured Social Security Disability, Medicaid, and other entitlements for that over 100 individuals with development disabilities
  • Fixed electronic Microsoft Office and PDF documents for accessibility with persons with Disabilities (Section 508)

Why is web development the next step in your career?

It all comes down to creation. I am at my happiest when I am creating something.
