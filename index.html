More About Me

Recently, Jon became a web developer by completing an intensive web development training. He is interested in freelance projects, or a full-time Junior Developer position. As someone with a strong visual sense, he is especially interested in writing code for front-end development.

My Resume


Why do you create webpages and edit videos?

Because I have a lifelong love for creating things.

When I was a child, I used to write short stories, create my own board games, and build communities with Legos. As I grew up, I listened to songs and imagined stories within them that could be told with video clips of popular movies and TV shows. I felt compelled to tell my stories so I became a video editor. My work expanded and includes professional videos used in trainings by the US Department of Homeland Security.

Recently, I learned web development because I wanted another outlet to express my creativity.

How did you become a video editor?

In 2007, I taught myself how to use iMovie and created my first video, A Tribute to Women in Film, which I scored with Bob Dylan's Lay Lady Lay.

While satisifed with my first production, I later saw things I wanted to change. At the same time, I started experimenting more and more with iMovie's features and adapted a style of editing that was in sync with the rhythm of the music. With each successive video I created, I used new features and tightened how I used cuts and transitions.

In 2009, I was hired for my first professional job. Whether I create a professional video for a client or a personal video of my own ideas, I find the process a labor of love and take great satisfaction when I see the final product!

How did you train to become a web developer?

I completed CareerFoundry's Web Development program. Through this program, I first learned how to create front-end websites using HTML, CSS, and Javascript. Then I progressed into back-end development using Ruby on Rails and created my own dynamic web application: New Mexico's Finest Peppers. Personally, I prefer front-end development as I am a visual person and love creating the "look" of a website; however, I was amazed when I saw code I programmed into the back-end of my application perform incredible functions.

What other work have you performed?

I've had a very diverse career. In eleven years, I've held the following jobs:


  • Fixed electronic Microsoft Office and PDF documents for accessibility with persons with disabilities (Section 508)
  • Secured Social Security Disability, Medicaid, and other entitlements for that over 100 individuals with development disabilities
  • Served as a case manager for 16 men with severe mental illness. Monitored my clients' psychiatric functioning and ensured that they received their medications, entitlements, and proper medical & psychiatric treatment
  • Covered night shifts at a psychiatric crisis center

Why is web development the next step in your career?

In 2012, I took a job as an on-site contractor at the US Depatment of Homeland Security's Office of Accessible Systems and Technology. After getting trained on Section 508 standards and how they apply to electronic documents, I became intrigued by how Section 508 applies to websites.


One day, a colleague opened up a text editor containing code for my office's task management application, made minor changes, and reloaded the application. I was amazed to see how everything the application used to function came from the lines of code in the text editor. It was then that I decided that my next endeavor would be to learn how to write code to create websites.


To me, learning code is the natural next step for learning how to create something beautiful and interesting. It all comes down to creation. I am at my happiest when I am creating something.
